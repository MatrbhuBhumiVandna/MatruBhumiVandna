About Matru Bhumi Vandana Trust

Our Vision

To establish a spiritual and cultural epicenter in Morbi, Gujarat, that:

  • Honors our motherland through the world's largest Bharat Mata Temple.
  • Revives ancient Indian wisdom through a modern Gurukul system.
  • Becomes a global beacon for nationalism and Sanatan Dharma values.
  • Preserves and propagates the principles of Arya Samaj for contemporary society.

Our Mission

1. Temple Development

  • Construct the world's largest Bharat Mata Temple, symbolizing national pride and devotion.
  • Create a sacred space uniting all Indians in reverence for our motherland.

2. Gurukul Education

  • Establish a state-of-the-art Gurukul blending Vedic knowledge with modern education.
  • Develop future leaders deeply rooted in Indian culture, values, and nationalism.
  • Offer residential programs focusing on character building, discipline, and spirituality.

3. Cultural Preservation

  • Promote Arya Samaj principles through daily rituals, discourses, and community programs.
  • Organize nationalist and cultural events celebrating India's heritage and pride.
  • Create a living museum showcasing India's spiritual and freedom struggle history.

4. Community Engagement

  • Conduct youth programs to instill patriotism and social responsibility.
  • Offer free classes in Sanskrit, Vedic mathematics, and Indian philosophy.
  • Develop outreach programs to schools and colleges across Gujarat.

5. National Integration

  • Host interfaith dialogues promoting unity in diversity.
  • Celebrate Indian festivals with traditional fervor and cultural significance.
  • Create platforms showcasing India's rich cultural and spiritual legacy.

Core Values

Nationalism

Fostering love for Bharat and its cultural heritage.

Spirituality

Promoting Sanatan Dharma and Arya Samaj principles.

Education

Blending ancient wisdom with modern knowledge.

Service

Encouraging selfless contribution to society and nation-building.

Unity

Bringing people together beyond caste, creed, and religion.

Who We Are

Founded in 2017, Matru Bhumi Vandana Trust started with a dedicated team of nine visionaries, united by a common goal - to make Morbi proud by building the world's biggest Bharat Mata Temple and reviving the Gurukul/Vedic education system.

Janta Raja Natak-2025

From 2nd May 2025 8:00 PM to 8th May 2025

We are organizing Janta Raja Natak-2025 to raise funds for building the world's biggest Bharat Mata Temple in Morbi, Gujarat. This historical play depicts the glorious history of India and the sacrifices of our freedom fighters.

The event will feature renowned artists and performers from across the country, bringing to life the stories of our brave patriots.

Recent Events

March 23rd - Shaheed Diwas Program

March 23, 2023 Morbi, Gujarat

Every year on March 23rd, we organize a special program to honor the sacrifices of Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev, and Rajguru. This year's event was particularly memorable with participation from over 500 community members.

The program included patriotic songs, speeches about the freedom struggle, and a reenactment of important moments from our history. Students from local schools participated in essay and painting competitions on the theme of patriotism.

Gurukul Foundation Stone Ceremony

January 15, 2023 Morbi, Gujarat

We laid the foundation stone for our modern Gurukul that will blend traditional Vedic education with contemporary learning. The ceremony was attended by prominent spiritual leaders and educationists from across Gujarat.

The Gurukul will focus on character building, spiritual growth, and academic excellence, creating future leaders rooted in Indian values. Construction is expected to be completed by mid-2024.

View Gallery

Vedic Mathematics Workshop

December 10, 2022 Morbi, Gujarat

A two-day workshop on Vedic Mathematics was conducted for students and teachers from local schools. The workshop demonstrated ancient Indian techniques for fast calculations that are still relevant today.

Over 200 participants learned methods that can simplify complex mathematical operations. The positive response has encouraged us to plan regular monthly workshops on this and other traditional Indian knowledge systems.
